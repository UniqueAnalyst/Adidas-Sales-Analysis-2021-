## Project Overview
This project analyzes Adidas’ 2021 U.S. sales data using Microsoft Power BI. The goal was to evaluate sales performance, profitability, regional trends, and customer purchasing behavior to uncover actionable business insights.

---

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Power BI
- Data Cleaning & Modeling
- Data Visualization

---

## Dataset Summary
- **Total Sales:** $717.8M  
- **Units Sold:** ~2M  
- **States Covered:** 50  
- **Regions:** 5  
- **Online Transactions:** 224K  

---

## Key KPIs
- Average Operating Profit: $34.43K  
- Average Operating Margin: 0.42  
- Top Product by Profit: Men’s Street Footwear  
- Lowest Product by Margin: Men’s Athletic Footwear  

---

## Key Insights

### Product Performance
- Men’s Street Footwear generated $209M in sales and $82.8M in operating profit.
- Men’s Athletic Footwear showed weak margins despite strong sales volume.

### Sales Channels
- Online: 50.7%
- Outlet: 31.3%
- In-store: 18%

### Regional Performance
- West Region led with $269.94M in sales.
- Northeast and Southeast followed closely.

### Seasonal Trends
- Sales peaked in July and August.
- Lowest performance occurred in March.
- Strong rebound in December.

---

## Recommendations
- Invest more in high-margin lifestyle footwear.
- Optimize pricing and costs for low-margin athletic products.
- Strengthen online sales strategy.
- Focus marketing efforts on top-performing regions.
- Use seasonal insights for inventory planning.

---

## Conclusion
The analysis shows Adidas’ strong revenue performance is driven by lifestyle products, online channels, and key regions. However, margin optimization remains a major opportunity. Aligning product strategy with profitability insights can significantly enhance overall performance.
